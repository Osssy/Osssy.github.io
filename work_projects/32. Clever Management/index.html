INDUSTRIAL ELECTRICAL EQUIPMENT

The electrical equipment of industrial buildings is a complex of electrical systems mounted in industrial buildings in order to ensure the smooth operation of units and lighting fixtures necessary for an uninterrupted production process. The electrical equipment of industrial buildings performs another important function - ensuring the safety of employees.

Electrical equipment for industrial buildings include:
  • electrical principle units;
  • apparatuses and devices of the power supply network, guaranteeing the proper functioning of artificial lighting, household electrical appliances; ventilation, heating systems and water supply systems;
  • industrial vehicles.

ELECTRICAL APPARATUS

An electrical apparatus is an electrotechnical device designed to control (measure, change, regulate) the electrical and non-electrical parameters of various technical systems and their components, as well as to protect them from overloads at different operating conditions. Such electrical equipment is used in ballasts, protection systems for electrical networks used in various production processes, vehicles, automation systems, communications, etc.

Depending on the main function performed, electrical devices are divided into six groups:

1. Regulatory apparatus

Used to regulate according to a given law or to maintain at a given level the values ​​of certain parameters (for example, electric stabilizers).

2. Launch-regulatory apparatus

Designed for starting and controlling electric drives, as well as for managing energy consumers.

3. Measuring electrical apparatus

Designed to measure large values ​​of currents and voltages using standard measuring instruments.

4. Electrical control apparatus

They are used to measure and control the set parameters (current, voltage, pressure, temperature). This group includes relays and sensors.

5. Limiting apparatus

They serve to limit short-circuit currents (reactors, fuses) and overvoltages (arresters).

6. Switching electric devices

Designed for switching electrical circuits (turning them on and off) under normal operating conditions.

ELECTRICAL MACHINES

Electric machines are called electromechanical energy converters. Electric machines include all types of electric generators, electric motors, and transformers. If electrical energy is converted into mechanical work and heat, then the electric machine is an electric motor; when non-electric forms of energy are converted into electrical energy and heat, then the electric machine is an electric generator; when the electric energy of one type is transformed into the electric energy of another type, then the electric machine is an electromechanical converter (transformer). Electric machines have the principle of reversibility: the same machine can operate both in electric motor mode and in generator mode. An electric machine consists of a fixed part - a stator and a moving part - a rotor.

Electric machines are classified by:

1. Type of electrical voltage

direct voltage, alternating voltage, single-phase, three-phase

2. Destination

electric motors, generators, converters, etc.

3. Correlation of rotor speed and stator magnetic field

synchronous and asynchronous

4. Constructive execution:

  • cooling method (forced, hydrogen, air, natural);
  • mounting method (on flanges, bearing racks, legs,);
  • a way of protection from the environment (explosion-proof, closed, protected, open, etc.).
