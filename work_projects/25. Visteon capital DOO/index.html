Wholesale trade

The role of wholesale trade in the economy

Wholesale trade is any activity that sells goods and services to those who purchase them either for further use (processing, sewing), or resale.

In wholesale trade, goods are purchased in large quantities and in large volumes.

The market space includes not only direct producers and consumers of marketable products, but also a significant number of active intermediate links establishing business relations between them. These links include wholesale and intermediary organizations that provide essential services to both producers and end-users of products.

Wholesale trade is the most important link that accelerates the process of product distribution through distribution channels.

The material and technical base of wholesale trade was created over several decades. In recent years, there has been a sharp reduction in the volume of wholesale operations. In the market conditions, wholesale enterprises found themselves in a crisis situation, much was destroyed. Wholesale trade was forced to engage in new activities (retail, manufacturing, renting out warehouses, etc.), change specialization, introduce new forms of organizing wholesale trade (associations, associations, etc.).

Therefore, the rational organization and improvement of wholesale trade is one of the most important tasks. The wholesale link determines the direction of commodity flows, converts the production assortment into a commercial one and acts as a conductor of a huge mass of goods to the consumer market.

Necessity and wholesale functions

All this allows both manufacturers and retailers to turn to wholesale services. The need for wholesale is associated with several factors:

  • uneven distribution of industrial enterprises producing individual groups and names of consumer goods. This necessitates a wide commodity exchange between enterprises located in different regions of the country;
  • natural and economic-geographical conditions of production, concentration of production in certain regions of the country;
  • many consumer goods are produced by numerous industrial enterprises, therefore it is necessary to involve all these dispersed resources in the commodity circulation and to assist industrial enterprises in marketing their products;
  • specialization of industrial enterprises in the production of consumer goods with a narrow production range. For retail enterprises, not only the assortment is limited, but also the volume of delivered goods during the purchase.

Necessity for the functioning of wholesale trade is caused by the fact that the direct sale of goods by manufacturing enterprises to the population is practically difficult, since they produce goods that are sold in various cities and towns. Therefore, trade in consumer goods is carried out in two stages:

  • at the first, initial stage, goods are sold by manufacturers to trading enterprises (wholesale) or trading enterprises to each other;
  • at the second, final stage, retailers sell goods to the public (retail).

In wholesale trade, goods can be sold two or more times — first at the regional, then at the local level.

Wholesale trade is a form of relations between enterprises, organizations, in which the economic relations for the supply of products are formed by the parties independently.

Wholesale trade determines the structure and direction of commodity flows. It acts as an intermediary in the market between industry and retail.

The development goals of the wholesale trade are:

  • creating a developed structure of distribution channels;
  • maintaining the necessary intensity of trade flows;
  • formation of reserve sources of financial support for the process of product distribution.

Wholesale trade plays the role of an active conductor of domestic goods and the movement and accumulation of a sufficiently large mass of imported goods in space and in time to the domestic consumer market. It forces domestic manufacturers to improve the quality of their goods of the day of gaining a solid place in the competition.

The typical tasks of the wholesale trade are:

  • Search for suppliers of goods for retailers and other buyers;
  • purchase of large consignments of goods from manufacturers;
  • increase in the number of stages of intermediate users of products;
  • the formation of the trade assortment and its adaptation to the requirements of intermediate and final consumers;
  • pursuing a policy of timely updating and improving the quality of goods;
  • providing manufacturers of sales of their goods;
  • market research for product manufacturers and retailers;
  • information service;
  • risk taking when handling goods.

Thus, manufacturers and retailers have every reason to use wholesale services.

Wholesale trade is an important lever for maneuvering material resources, contributes to the reduction of surplus stocks of products at all levels of commodity circulation and elimination of commodity shortages, and takes part in the formation of industry and regional commodity markets.

Through wholesale trade the consumer’s influence on the producer is intensified, and real possibilities for regulating supply and demand appear. Market demand forms, on the one hand, the flow of information from the consumer through trade to the producer, and on the other hand, the note of goods from the producer through trade to the consumer.

In this process, the coherence of both flows must be achieved, and wholesale trade plays a decisive role. It is designed to serve as a regulator of market demand and adapt to consumer requirements, which ensures the filling of the market with goods.

At the macro level, wholesale performs the following functions:

  • integrating (to ensure the relationship between partners in the supply of products, to find distribution channels);
  • estimated (by determining the level of socially necessary labor costs through pricing);
  • organizing and regulating (to ensure the rational construction and harmonious functioning of economic systems with the help of impulses that stimulate structural changes).

Wholesale Features:

  • distribution - the purchase of goods of a specialized production assortment from various manufacturers and the assortment of the assortment taking into account differentiated demand (the formation of a trade assortment by sorting goods);
  • accumulation and storage - accumulation of the time interval between different seasons;
  • organization of goods distribution - overcoming the space when the producer-supplier and the retail company are at a sufficient distance and shipping is carried out in large quantities (in this case, the wholesale company centrally delivers the goods to retail enterprises in small quantities);
  • control - ensuring the quality of goods delivered to the retail enterprise;
  • market regulation - price equalization through the purchase of large quantities of goods and the receipt of significant discounts on purchased goods;
  • credit - financing of retail enterprises when goods are delivered without payment and there is a time interval between delivery and payment;
  • marketing - research and market development through advertising, marketing, pricing system due to attractive product packaging, etc .;
  • provision of storage and transportation - provides rhythm and guaranteed delivery of goods to the retail network in small batches.

These functions of wholesale trade at the micro level are transformed into the various functions of wholesale trading enterprises.

Wholesale functions in relation to suppliers:

  • concentration of commercial activity;
  • supporting the process of transfer of ownership of goods;
  • investment support for the process of product distribution;
  • minimization of commercial risk;
  • market research.

Wholesale functions in relation to wholesale buyers:

  • assessment of needs and demand;
  • transformation of industrial assortment into commercial;
  • bridging the spatial gap and economic integration of the territory;
  • stockpiling and storage of inventories;
  • packing, packing;
  • completion, bringing the goods to the required quality;
  • goods delivery;
  • lending;
  • information and consulting services.

The performance of these functions forms the basis of the commercial work of wholesale.

Automotive Parts

What automotive parts do you need

Today, nearly a billion cars travel on the roads of the world. Several tens of millions of cars are added to them annually. They all need auto parts. This huge need has brought to life a system that spans the entire globe, where auto parts are manufactured, transported, stored, distributed in markets, and sold. Ultimately, auto parts are installed on the car.

Thousands of the most diverse enterprises located on almost all continents produce auto parts. These auto parts have a huge range and are made for different car systems. They are mainly made of metal, and should ensure the trouble-free functioning of the engine, chassis, transmission of the car, its brake system.

A significant part of the spare parts is made of plastics and rubber. Such auto parts are used in depreciation systems, car interior equipment, as seals for various devices where sealing and compaction are required.

A large place in the total volume of spare parts for cars is occupied by body parts. Indeed, not every body damage can be eliminated by straightening and other repair work. Therefore, production has been organized that make wings, trunk lids, doors and other car body parts.

Great demand for auto parts for electric cars. These are numerous wires and their entire harnesses, various electric motors, generators, relays and other electrical devices. Modern cars also require computer parts, which are involved in the management of various car systems.

If we add to this a huge assortment of various accessories necessary for the car, starting from the pump and keys and ending with jacks, then we can make sure that the market of components and other necessary things that the machines need is inexhaustible.

What is building materials

Building material is any material used for construction purpose such as materials for house building. Wood, cement, aggregates, metals, bricks, concrete, clay are the most common type of building material used in construction. Timber

What is timber

Timber is one of the most useful and important material for constructions. Selecting timber is not an easy task, because timber has different types out of which selecting the right material is an important key. Timber is an expensive material to be incorporated in a building for different purpose therefore it should necessarily be strong, tough and durable. Timber doors or windows and etc. contribute a lot in the beautification and overall look of interiors. Timber is used in doors, windows, cabinet, cupboards, shelves, tables and railings etc. Timber is also popularly used in the form of plywood & raw wood. Products like ply blocks and ply boards. Heavy patterned doors and windows are made of solid wood/Timber to provide the strength, toughness and durability.

Type of timber to be used for right purpose is important because if timber used in construction is of low quality then this may need replacement. While selecting timber one should consider its quality aspect as timber must be free from decay like rotten, fungi and termite. Following are some essential tips and guidelines to know about timber selection for buildings:

  • Teakwood is a most appropriate timber to be used in the construction of buildings.
  • Sal is another timber material identified as much stronger and quite harder than teakwood used in building purposes.
  • Deodar is one of a strongest conifer with less strength and weight than teakwood.
  • Hardwood is another form of wood which is treated thoroughly before use as it is not durable as much as teakwood or deodar.
  • Ply board is used in buildings for various purposes.
  • Particle board is made of agro waste, cellulose etc and blended with adhesive to make into a solid board.
